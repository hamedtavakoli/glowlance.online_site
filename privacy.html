Privacy

Simple, clear privacy information for VocabGlow.

This page explains how VocabGlow handles data in plain language and why the extension aims to stay lightweight and respectful of user privacy.

Privacy summary

VocabGlow is built to help with vocabulary learning while browsing, without turning users into analytics data. The goal is to keep the experience helpful, focused, and easy to trust.

  • No analytics
  • No tracking
  • No personal data collection
  • No third-party data sharing

Permissions explained simply

Browser permissions may be needed so the extension can read selected words, display helper information, save vocabulary, highlight saved words, and show reminders where supported. These permissions are used to provide the extension's core functionality, not to profile users.

Local storage and sync storage

Saved words, preferences, and related settings may be stored locally in the browser or in browser extension storage, depending on how the extension is configured. Where applicable, this keeps the data close to the user's own browser environment instead of collecting it as personal data on a separate service.

Dictionary and translation support

VocabGlow may use dictionary or translation-related APIs to provide meanings, pronunciation, examples, synonyms, or translated results. These services are used to return the requested language support and are not intended for analytics or advertising.

What VocabGlow does not do

VocabGlow does not aim to collect personal information, build advertising profiles, or share user information with third parties. The focus is on providing vocabulary help while keeping the experience lightweight and straightforward.
